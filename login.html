What is UIDAI?

The Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) was set up by the Indian government under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY). It is responsible for issuing Aadhaar numbers to residents of India — a 12-digit unique identity.

What is Aadhaar?

Aadhaar is a unique 12-digit number that UIDAI randomly assigns to each resident. It verifies identity and confirms address.

myAadhaar/UIDAI: Mission & Objective

The goal is to empower citizens with a unique digital identity, ensuring services and benefits are delivered transparently.

Objectives

  • Robust identity verification.
  • Easy access to Aadhaar services.
  • Privacy and data security.
